What is Hydroponics?

Plants don't need soil.
They need nutrients.

Hydroponics is the science of growing plants in water infused with minerals — no earth required. Roots get exactly what they need, when they need it. The result? Crops that grow up to 3× faster, use 90% less water than traditional farming, and can thrive anywhere from a city apartment to a polar research station.

By 2050, the world will need to feed 10 billion people. Hydroponics is one of the most promising answers — bringing fresh food to food deserts, reducing agricultural land use, and putting growing power back in the hands of everyday people.
